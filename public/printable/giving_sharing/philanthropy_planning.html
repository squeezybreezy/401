Philanthropy Planning: The Joy of Giving

All Ages

Hello, Generous Heart!

In "401Kid," Walter Clarke teaches that giving is one of the Five Faces of a Dollar. When we share our money, time, and talents with others, we not only help them but also enrich our own lives. This activity will help you create your own philanthropy plan—a strategy for how you want to make a positive difference in the world!

Why Giving Matters

Giving to others is important because:

  • It helps people, animals, or causes that need support
  • It connects us to our community and the wider world
  • It teaches us gratitude for what we have
  • It brings joy and purpose to our lives
  • It creates a positive cycle of generosity
"We make a living by what we get, but we make a life by what we give." — Winston Churchill

The Impact of Giving

Even small donations can make a big difference! Here are some examples:

$5 can provide:

  • A meal for someone who is hungry
  • School supplies for a child
  • Vaccines for animals at a shelter
  • Seeds to plant trees

$20 can provide:

  • A backpack of weekend food for a child
  • Clean water for a family for a month
  • Medicine for sick animals
  • Books for a classroom

Your time can provide:

  • Companionship for elderly people
  • Help at an animal shelter
  • Cleanup of parks or beaches
  • Tutoring for younger students

2. Why do you think Walter Clarke included "Give" as one of the Five Faces of a Dollar?

Note for Parents:

This philanthropy planning activity connects to Walter Clarke's emphasis on giving as one of the Five Faces of a Dollar in 401Kid. Teaching children to be intentional about giving helps them develop empathy, gratitude, and a sense of purpose with their money.

To enhance this activity:

  • Share your own family's giving traditions and values
  • Help your child research organizations that support causes they care about
  • Consider setting up a "giving jar" where a portion of allowance or earnings goes
  • Look for age-appropriate volunteer opportunities you can do together
  • Discuss how giving doesn't always mean money—time and talents are valuable too

As Clarke notes in the book, teaching children to give helps them understand that money is a tool for making a positive difference in the world, not just for personal consumption. This mindset fosters a healthy relationship with money and connects financial literacy to values and purpose.
