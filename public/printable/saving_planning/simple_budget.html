The Five Faces of a Dollar

In the book "401Kid," Walter Clarke talks about the five different ways we can use our money:

  1. Save: Keep money for future needs and wants
  2. Spend: Use money for things we need and want now
  3. Invest: Use money to make more money grow over time
  4. Give: Share money with others who need help
  5. Earn: Get more money by working or creating value

Budget Decision Time!

Let's practice making budget decisions:

Scenario 1: Your grandma gives you $5 for your birthday. Your friend invites you to get ice cream that costs $5, but you've been saving for a $20 toy.

Option A: Spend all $5 on ice cream with your friend

Option B: Save the $5 toward your toy and suggest a free activity with your friend

Circle your choice. Why did you choose this option?

Scenario 2: You earned $10 doing chores. You want to buy a $7 book, but you also promised to donate $2 to help animals at the shelter.

Option A: Buy the book now and skip the donation

Option B: Donate $2 as promised and save $8 to buy the book later

Circle your choice. Why did you choose this option?

Note for Parents:

This budgeting activity introduces children to Walter Clarke's "Five Faces of a Dollar" concept from 401Kid. By creating a simple budget, children begin to understand that money has different purposes and that planning helps them achieve their financial goals.

To enhance this activity:

  • Use real money to physically divide into different jars or envelopes
  • Discuss your family's approach to budgeting in age-appropriate terms
  • Help your child understand that budgeting isn't about restriction but about making intentional choices
  • Point out that the "earn" category is about planning ways to increase income
  • Revisit the budget regularly to reinforce the habit of financial planning

As Clarke emphasizes in the book, financial discipline starts with understanding that every dollar has a purpose. This activity helps children develop that mindset from an early age.
