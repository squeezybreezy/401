Needs vs. Wants: Making Smart Choices

Ages 5-7

Hello, Money Explorer!

When we spend money, we need to make smart choices. Some things we buy are things we NEED to live and be healthy. Other things are things we WANT because they're fun or make us happy. Let's learn the difference!

What's the Difference?

NEEDS are things we must have to live safely and healthily. We can't go without our needs for very long.

WANTS are things that are nice to have and make us happy, but we can live without them if we have to.

Let's Talk About It!

  1. Why is food a need but candy is a want?
  2. Can something be both a need AND a want? Give an example.
  3. If you had $10, would you spend it on a need or a want? Why?
  4. What happens if we spend all our money on wants and forget about our needs?

Note for Parents:

This activity introduces the fundamental concept of distinguishing between needs and wants - a cornerstone of financial literacy. As Walter Clarke emphasizes in 401Kid, teaching children to prioritize needs over wants helps build financial discipline early.

Extend this activity by:

  • Taking your child shopping and asking them to identify needs vs. wants as you shop
  • Creating a simple budget together that allocates money for both needs and wants
  • Discussing how saving money helps us prepare for future needs
  • Talking about how some wants can become needs in certain situations (like a car might be a want in a city with good public transportation, but a need in a rural area)

Remember that these early conversations about money create the foundation for lifelong financial habits.
