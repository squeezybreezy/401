Money Matters Coloring Page

Ages 5-7

Hello, Money Explorer!

Coloring is fun! Today, we're going to color a picture about saving money. While you color, think about why saving money is important and what you might want to save for!

Color the piggy bank and coins. What colors will you use?

Fun Facts About Saving Money:

  • Piggy banks got their name because people used to save money in jars made of clay called "pygg"
  • Some people save money to buy something special they really want
  • Others save money for emergencies or to help others
  • When you save money in a bank, they give you extra money called "interest"
  • Even saving a little bit of money every week can add up to a lot over time!

Let's Talk About It!

  1. What would you like to save your money for?
  2. Where do you keep your money when you save it?
  3. How does it feel when you save up for something and finally get to buy it?
  4. Why is it important to save some of our money instead of spending it all?

Note for Parents:

This coloring activity introduces the concept of saving in a fun, engaging way. As Walter Clarke emphasizes in 401Kid, early positive associations with financial concepts help children develop healthy money habits.

While your child colors, use this opportunity to discuss:

  • The importance of saving as one of the "Five Faces of a Dollar" mentioned in the book
  • Your family's approach to saving money
  • How delayed gratification (saving for something instead of buying it immediately) builds character
  • Consider setting up a real piggy bank or savings jar if you haven't already

Remember that these early, positive conversations about money create the foundation for lifelong financial habits.
