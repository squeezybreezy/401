The Magic of Compound Interest

Ages 10-13

Hello, Future Investor!

Did you know that money can grow all by itself? It's like having a money tree that gets bigger and bigger over time! This is called "compound interest," and it's one of the most powerful tools for building wealth. Let's explore how it works!

What is Compound Interest?

Compound interest is when you earn interest not just on the money you save, but also on the interest you've already earned. It's like a snowball rolling down a hill, getting bigger and bigger as it collects more snow.

Here's the magic formula:

Money + Time + Interest = Much More Money!

The Magic Penny Experiment

Imagine you have two choices:

  1. Receive $1,000,000 (one million dollars) right now, OR
  2. Receive one penny on day 1, two pennies on day 2, four pennies on day 3, and so on - doubling every day for 30 days.

Which would you choose? Let's see what happens with the penny:

Note for Parents:

This activity introduces the powerful concept of compound interest, which Walter Clarke emphasizes in 401Kid as a key to building wealth over time. Understanding compound interest helps children see the value of starting to save and invest early.

To enhance this activity:

  • Use an online compound interest calculator to explore different scenarios with your child
  • Consider opening a small investment account for your child to experience compound growth firsthand
  • Discuss the "Rule of 72" - divide 72 by the interest rate to estimate how many years it takes for money to double
  • Connect this concept to the "invest" face of the Five Faces of a Dollar framework
  • Emphasize that time is the most powerful factor in compound growth - starting early is more important than the amount

As Clarke notes in the book, teaching children about investing and compound interest helps them develop patience and a long-term perspective on money - crucial traits for financial success.
