Hello, Young Entrepreneur!

Did you know that you can create your own business? Entrepreneurs are people who see problems and create solutions that help others. They create value and earn money by solving problems. Let's brainstorm some business ideas that you could start!

What is an Entrepreneur?

An entrepreneur is someone who:

  • Sees problems that need solving
  • Creates products or services that help people
  • Takes risks and tries new things
  • Works hard to make their ideas successful
  • Earns money by creating value for others

In "401Kid," Walter Clarke talks about how important it is to "think like an owner." Entrepreneurs are owners who create their own opportunities!

Note for Parents:

This entrepreneurial brainstorming activity helps children "think like an owner" - a key concept in Walter Clarke's 401Kid. By encouraging children to identify problems and create solutions, you're helping them develop an entrepreneurial mindset that will serve them throughout life.

To enhance this activity:

  • Share stories of young entrepreneurs who started successful businesses
  • Discuss businesses in your community and how they solve problems
  • Help your child think through the practicality of their ideas without discouraging creativity
  • Consider helping them start a small version of their business idea
  • Talk about how entrepreneurship connects to the "earn" and "create value" concepts from the book
