Hello, Value Creator!

In "401Kid," Walter Clarke teaches that money is a measurement of the value you create for others. The more value you create, the more money you can earn! Let's explore how you can create value and turn it into earning opportunities.

What is Value?

Value means making something better, easier, or more enjoyable for someone else. When you create value, you're solving a problem or fulfilling a need that someone has.

Solving Problems

Finding solutions to things that bother people or make their lives difficult.

Example: Creating a dog-walking service for busy neighbors

Saving Time

Helping people do things faster or more efficiently.

Example: Organizing someone's bookshelf or toy collection

Creating Joy

Making something that brings happiness or entertainment.

Example: Making handmade greeting cards or artwork

Teaching Skills

Sharing knowledge that helps others learn something new.

Example: Teaching younger kids how to play a sport

Note for Parents:

This value creation activity directly connects to Walter Clarke's core message in 401Kid that "money follows value." By helping children understand that earning money starts with creating value for others, you're instilling an entrepreneurial mindset that will serve them throughout life.

To enhance this activity:

  • Help your child identify their unique strengths and how these can translate into value for others
  • Discuss examples of value creation in everyday life and in businesses you patronize
  • Consider helping your child implement their value creation plan as a small business
  • Talk about how creating value connects to the "earn" aspect of the Five Faces of a Dollar
  • Emphasize that creating value isn't just about making money—it's about making a positive difference

As Clarke emphasizes in the book, teaching children to create value rather than just expect money instills a growth mindset and builds the foundation for financial independence.
